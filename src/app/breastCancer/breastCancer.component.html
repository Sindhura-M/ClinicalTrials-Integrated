What is breast cancer ?

Cancer occurs when cells grow in an abnormal way and multiple uncontrollably, invading healthy tissue.

Normally, cells in our body are replaced through the development of new cells. This process is regulated by our genes. Sometimes, the genes are abnormal (mutated) meaning this process is disrupted and the cells may grow and divide rapidly, forming a cancerous or malignant tumour. The cancer cells may spread to surrounding tissue or other parts of the body.

Breast cancer is the abnormal growth of cells lining the breast lobules (milk producing glands) and breast ducts (passages that drain milk from the lobules towards the nipple). The cells grow uncontrollably, forming an invasive breast cancer. The cells have the potential to spread to other parts of the body. There are different types of breast cancer, which may require different treatments. The three main subtypes of breast cancer include hormone receptor positive breast cancer, HER2 receptor positive breast cancer and triple negative breast cancer. Breast cancer can occur in women and men.
